"Trust The Process" Did It Really Work?

A fan holds a "Trust The Process" sign at a 76ers game. (Mithcell Left / Getty Images)

In 2013, the Philadelphia 76ers made a decision that would change the franchise forever. General manager Sam Hinkie began deliberately losing games to collect high draft picks, a strategy fans came to know as 'The Process.' The data below tracks whether that gamble actually paid off.

Looking at the data, the answer depends on what you were expecting. The 76ers rebuilt from one of the worst stretches in NBA history, turned Joel Embiid into a franchise cornerstone, and became consistent playoff contenders. But the championship they promised fans never came. So did the Process work? It worked just enough to give Philadelphia hope. Just not enough to give them the banner.
